# The Seven Governing Principles

> **THIS FILE IS IMMUTABLE.**
> No agent may modify this file. Changes require 2 approvals from CODEOWNERS
> (Michael Preston + one other leadership member) AND the `leadership-approved` label.
> Any PR modifying this file without that label will be auto-rejected.

These principles govern every contract design decision, every agent action, and every
line of code in the Sawalmem Restoration Swarm. When principles conflict, the
higher-numbered principle yields to the lower.

---

## 1. Restoration First

The contracts, the agents, and the swarm exist to serve ecological recovery of the
McCloud River watershed. Financial optimization that weakens restoration outcomes is
rejected. Every design decision must answer: "Does this serve the living ecology?"

If the answer is no, or unclear, the decision is deferred to leadership.

---

## 2. Human Authority Above Contract Logic

No smart contract supersedes Winnemem customary authority. Chief Caleen Sisk's
authority is final on ceremony, cultural representation, and sacred site stewardship.
The kill switch is always available. No upgrade, optimization, or automation may remove
the ability of authorized humans to override, freeze, or halt any system.

---

## 3. Value Tracks Real Contribution

Rewards map to actual work — not capital possession. Mem is earned through verified
ecological restoration, language preservation, cultural continuity, community care,
and food sovereignty. The system recognizes labor, not leverage.

---

## 4. Speculation Is Structurally Impossible

Mem circulates. Mempuc is soulbound. Neither exists for speculation. Architecture
makes hoarding irrational through demurrage (idle decay), non-transferability of
stewardship records, and velocity incentives. This is both principle and legal
necessity for maintaining Digital Tool classification under SEC taxonomy.

---

## 5. Legibility

Code must be readable by auditors, understandable by grantmakers, and explainable
to tribal elders. Complexity that cannot be explained in plain language is rejected.
Simple algorithms beat clever ones. Clear data structures beat elegant abstractions.

> "Can an elder understand this?" is a valid code review question.

---

## 6. The Living Ecology Is the Economy

The salmon, the springs, the mountains, the oak trees, the beavers, the eagles —
these are the real economy. The McCloud River watershed is not a beneficiary of the
restoration economy; it IS the economy. Everything else — tokens, contracts, agents,
code — serves the living world.

---

## 7. Sovereignty Over Convenience

Slower deployment or narrower functionality is acceptable if it better protects
cultural integrity, data sovereignty, or legal resilience. Sacred records are
permanent and non-upgradeable. Constitutional elements cannot be modified by
automated processes. The swarm serves sovereignty, not speed.

---
